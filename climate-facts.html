Fun Facts about Climate Change

  • The Earth’s average temperature has risen about 1.2 degrees Celsius since the late 19th century.
  • Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction due to climate change, habitat loss, and other human impacts.
  • Sea levels are rising at an alarming rate due to melting ice caps and glaciers, affecting coastal communities worldwide.
  • A single tree can absorb approximately 48 pounds of carbon dioxide per year, helping to reduce greenhouse gases.
  • The Arctic is warming twice as fast as the rest of the planet, affecting global weather patterns.
  • Renewable energy sources, like wind and solar, have become more affordable and are essential to combating climate change.
  • Climate change can increase the intensity and frequency of extreme weather events, such as hurricanes and heatwaves.
  • The transportation sector is one of the largest sources of greenhouse gas emissions worldwide.
  • Food production is responsible for about 25% of global greenhouse gas emissions, highlighting the importance of sustainable agriculture.
  • Small actions, like reducing waste and conserving energy, can collectively make a significant impact on the environment.
